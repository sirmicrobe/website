Biography

Chris Marshall is a research assistant professor in the Department of Microbiology and Molecular Genetics at the University of Pittsburgh. His research interests include the evolution of antimicrobial resistance, eco-evolutionary dynamics, environmental microbiology, bioremediation, and bioelectrochemistry. Chris did his PhD in Hal May’s lab at MUSC studying extracellular electron transfer pathways in Gram positive bacteria powering microbial fuel cells and microbial electrosynthesis systems. Following his Phd, Chris won the prestigious Argonne Director’s Fellowship and worked with Ken Kemner and Jack Gilbert at Argonne National Laboratory and University of Chicago. There, Chris analyzed microbiomes from soils, sediments, trees, sea sponges, and other ecosystems and helped develop metabolic models of complex microbial communities. Chris currently studies pathogen evolution in vitro and in vivo in the lab of Vaughn Cooper.
